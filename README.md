# OperatingPlatforms
Operating Platforms Portfolio Prep for SNHU CS-230

# Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
The Gaming Room client is a company that wanted to push their game, Draw It or Lose It, from Android to a web-based application that can run on all mainstream devices. They wanted to design software that is scalable, online, and runs cross-platform among devices. 

# What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I think that I designed and followed requirements pretty well in this project as well as creating good design requirements to supplement the original requirements. In order to create a game that worked through a web browser, the best course of action is to create a server-client relationship between the client and users. I found that for that relationship, the best operating platform is to use linux for the server and design the structure of the game around the original Android application for the clients.

# What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
I found that the design document had a solid timeline for the progress of the project and a straight forward blueprint for creating the application and fulfilling the requirements of the client. The document also included the design restraits that helped mold and straighten out the future design of the project itself.

# If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
I would revise the section that compared and contrasted the different operating systems, especially since I feel that I didn't go into as full of detail as I could. By more thouroughly going through every operating platform, I could find out if Linux really is the optimal platform to use, or if the other platforms offer significant difference between the others.

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I interpreted The Gaming Room's needs as developing an application that works on all platforms, while also functionally working as a web-based application. It's important to consider the user's needs because having a web-based application works best with a server-client relationship, thus needing an operating platform for the server, while the web-based application can be programmed in all needed platforms. It's also important to keep note of their needs since ignoring requirements could lead to unsatisfactory product that the client would not want.

# How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
I approached designing the software by first gathering requirements from my client. I then created models and found the design restraints for those requirements. Then I created and executed any applications and design for those requirements to meet the needs of my client. I would use these strategies for all future projects and deisng software for the needs and requirements of my clients.
